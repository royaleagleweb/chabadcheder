The Program

Yes. The Pre-1A program is a complete school-year experience running from the start of the academic year through the end. It follows a consistent daily schedule and structured curriculum designed for childhood development.

This is a fully planned and operational Pre-1A class for the upcoming school year. While future expansion is a long-term goal, continuation beyond this year is not guaranteed and will depend on permission granted, planning, and the success of the first class.

The class will be led by experienced early childhood Morahs Mrs. Chaya Katz and Mrs. Mattie Schurder. They work within a structured educational framework and are supported by curriculum guidance aligned with established Chabad early education standards.

We use a structured Kriah program to build strong Hebrew reading skills step by step, along with a hands-on math curriculum focused on number sense and problem-solving. Learning also includes פרשת השבוע, ימים טובים, davening, and an interactive "Wonders of Hashem" science program — all taught in a warm, Chassidishe environment.

The day includes joyful davening and circle times, Kriah instruction, and math, along with engaging lessons in Parsha and Yomim Tovim. Students also participate in hands-on activities, projects, singing, and interactive science — creating a balanced day of structured learning and meaningful, enjoyable experiences.

Daily Operations

Student safety is a top priority. The school will have a dedicated security presence during school hours. Entry and dismissal are carefully structured through a supervised carpool system that runs in conjunction with the preschool's carpool line, ensuring a smooth pickup.

Drop-off and pickup are conducted through an organized carpool system designed to ensure safety, efficiency, and consistency. Full instructions will be provided prior to the start of the school year.

Students receive meals through the licensed preschool food program. Parents complete a separate food form that includes financial eligibility and allergy information.

Discipline & Support

Our discipline approach is caring and individualized, based on the principle of "chanoch lenar al pi darko" — חנוך לנער על פי דרכו. We use a conscious discipline framework that focuses on understanding each child, teaching self-regulation, and guiding behavior with respect — helping children grow both emotionally and in their character.

If a child experiences challenges, our staff will work in close partnership with parents to support the child with consistency, structure, and care. We view school and home as a team in supporting each child's development. If additional issues still arise, we will seek outside professional guidance to best help each child reach their potential.

Logistics & Contact

We are actively completing all logistical and operational steps to ensure readiness for the modular classroom. In the unlikely event of delays in our permitting, a backup plan of starting the school year at Chabad of Central Broward (a two-minute drive from shul) is in place, and families will be communicated with directly and promptly. Our goal remains to begin the year in the modular unit, and we plan to do so on time.
